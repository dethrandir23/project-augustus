# PROJECT TRADEFALL

## Game Concept

In this game, you manage a company. You can either choose from predefined scenario companies or create your own from scratch. Different starting templates are available: a large but heavily indebted corporation, a small low-capital startup, or other risk-based configurations.

---

## World and Economy

The world consists of **TradeNodes** — settlements such as villages, cities, ports, or desert towns.

Each TradeNode has its own:

* Population
* Production
* Consumption
* Resource profile

These characteristics vary based on their template. A small village does not consume and produce the same goods as a major port city.

If a settlement produces 3 units of wheat but requires 6 units for its population, the shortage becomes **market demand**. Likewise, if it produces iron ore but does not consume it, it will attempt to sell it on the market.

---

## Markets and Orderbook System

Each TradeNode belongs to a market. Markets maintain separate **orderbooks** for each good.

* Trading is dynamic
* Prices update based on the latest executed transactions
* Supply and demand directly shape the economy

If a settlement’s consumer needs are sufficiently met during a turn:

* Population grows
* Happiness increases
* Growth rate bonuses apply

If needs are unmet:

* Population declines
* Economic contraction may occur

---

## Company Management and Production

As a company, you build factories. To produce goods, you must:

* Hire workers
* Secure raw materials
* Establish logistics routes

You are responsible for selling goods in the most profitable markets.

To maximize profit, you may:

* Analyze markets
* Manipulate supply and demand
* Build low-cost logistics routes
* Maintain continuous supply chains
* Place low-priced buy orders for non-critical goods for instant execution

---

## Logistics and Risks

Trade routes can be attacked by pirates. Supply chains may be disrupted.

Risk management is a key part of gameplay. Poor planning can break your entire production chain.

---

## Stock Market and Finance

Upon reaching certain capital thresholds, a special event may allow your company to go public via IPO.

The financial system includes:

* Purchasing shares of other companies
* Orderbook-based stock trading
* News affecting share prices
* Creating shell companies to accumulate shares

A **reputation system** exists. Manipulation, sabotage, and fake news may generate short-term profit but can damage long-term credibility.

---

## Governments and War

The world contains multiple states that may go to war.

During wartime:

* Military goods increase in price
* Access to enemy markets may be restricted

You can sign monopoly agreements to dominate specific goods in specific markets. However, this may trigger lawsuits and legal challenges.

---

## Legal System

The game includes a legal framework covering:

* Workplace accidents
* Labor inspections
* Compensation lawsuits
* Anti-monopoly cases

Failure to implement proper reforms and safety measures may result in fines or legal consequences.

---

## Event System

A dynamic event system keeps the game unpredictable.

Example:

**Warehouse fire:**

* Prioritize employees → fewer worker losses, more inventory loss
* Prioritize goods → fewer inventory losses, more worker casualties

As employees may gain experience levels in future development, such decisions carry long-term consequences.

---

## Departments and Advisors

Your company includes departments such as:

* Trade
* Stock Market
* Logistics
* Human Resources

You can assign AI advisors with different star ratings.

* Higher-rated advisors make better decisions
* They demand higher salaries
* You can micromanage everything yourself or automate selected areas

For example, you may automate low-value goods trading while personally managing high-margin products in the late game.

---

## Conclusion

Your objective is to:

* Produce more
* Sell more profitably
* Build more factories
* Expand logistics infrastructure
* Invest in shares
* Grow an economic empire

However, poor decisions, negative events, wars, lawsuits, or stronger competitors may lead to bankruptcy.

Whether the story ends in triumph or collapse, it is your story.

At any point, you may retire with your accumulated wealth and conclude the campaign on your own terms.
